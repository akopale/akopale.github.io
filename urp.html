Sexual Assault & Domestic Violence News Coverage in Chicago

Spatial Patterns & Socioeconomic Correlates

Why?

While violent crime in general is a well studied topic, sexual assault and domestic violence are often thrown into that category even though they are inherently different than other types of violent crimes. I plan to look at these specific type of violent crimes, exploring their spatial patterns and socioeconomic correlates.


I plan to also look at how news media coverage - primarily major Chicago-area print news outlets - varies by where the crime took place.

Background

VIOLENT CRIME

-Rates have been decreasing significantly since the early 90s (1)

-Economic and social disadvantage are strong correlates (2, 3, 4)

-Perpetrator & victim are more likely to be male (1, 4)

-Fatal injuries are more likely if the perpetrator is male (1, 4)


DOMESTIC VIOLENCE & SEXUAL ASSAULT

-Victims are more likely to be female (1)

-Fatalities are more likely if the victim is female (1)


Data and Methods

This project includes data from the US Census Bureau (socioeconomic), Social Impact Research Center (socioeconomic), City of Chicago (crime), and the Chicago Justice Project (news coverage).


Below is an interactive map showing different socioeconomic variables of interest.


Census data to neighborhoods: calculate everything as counts in excel > polygon to centroid > spatially join to neighborhood shapefile > sum counts > divide by population to calculate rates

Crime & news data: spatially join point data to the newly-created shapefile > divide by population

Spatial analysis: Global Moran's I, Anselin Local Moran's I

Regression analysis: OLS in GeoDa, spatial lag or spatial error if necessary
